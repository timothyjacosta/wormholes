Restore Points

Wormholes keeps up to five recent restore points. Automatic snapshots are spaced out to avoid excessive storage use, and emergency snapshots are preserved before risky data operations.

Restore points are stored in this browser. Clearing all browser site data can still remove them.

Data Safety

Wormholes keeps your projects on this device. It does not sync them to an account or send them to a Wormholes server.

Important: Wormholes does not encrypt your browser data, folder files, or backups. Someone with access to them may be able to read or change them.

What Wormholes protects

Imports are checked before use. Saved writing is treated as content, not executable code. Supported builds block unexpected scripts and network access.

What Wormholes relies on

Your browser, device, browser profile, extensions, and file permissions must be trustworthy. Wormholes cannot protect data after someone gains control of them.

Restore point limits

Restore points help with recent mistakes, but they live in the browser. Clearing site data, private browsing, browser storage cleanup, or losing the device can remove them.

Backups and folders

App Data exports and backup folders are separate protection only when saved outside the browser profile. Folder access follows your device permissions, and files can be changed outside Wormholes.

Links and online services

External links open outside Wormholes. This build has no AI service or online sync.

Recommended: keep regular App Data exports in a location you control.

Keeping storage manageable

Images and long Literature documents usually use the most space. Remove items you no longer need, keep a portable App Data export before major cleanup, and reconnect the local folder when its values cannot be measured. Restore points are automatically limited to five.
